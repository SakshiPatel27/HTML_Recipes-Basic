Aloo Paratha



Aloo Paratha is a popular Indian flatbread stuffed with a spiced potato filling . The dough, made from whole wheat flour, is rolled out and filled with a mixture of mashed potatoes, onions, and a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The stuffed dough is then rolled into a flatbread and cooked on a griddle with a bit of oil or ghee until crispy and golden brown. Itâ€™s often enjoyed with yogurt, pickles, or a dollop of butter and is a staple in Punjab North Indian cuisine.


Ingredients

    For the Dough:-

  • Whole wheat flour: 2 cups
  • Water: As needed (usually about 1 cup)
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Oil or ghee: 1 tablespoon (optional, for softer dough)

    For the Filling:-

  • Potatoes: 2-3 medium, boiled and mashed
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped (optional)
  • Green chili: 1, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder: 1/2 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves): 2 tablespoons, chopped
  • Salt: To taste
  • Amchur (dry mango powder): 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for tanginess)

    Steps:-

    Preparation:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
    • Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead to form a soft, pliable dough. If using, add 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee to the dough for added softness.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a bowl, mix together the boiled and mashed potatoes, finely chopped onions (if using), chopped green chili (if using), coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, chopped cilantro, and salt.
    • Optionally, add 1/2 teaspoon of amchur (dry mango powder) for a tangy flavor.
    • Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

    Assembling and Cooking:

  1. Divide the Dough and Filling:
    • Divide the Dough and Filling:
    • Similarly, divide the potato filling into portions that match the number of dough balls.
  2. Roll Out the Paratha:
    • Take one dough ball and roll it into a small circle on a floured surface.
    • Place one portion of the potato filling in the center of the circle.
    • Gently bring the edges of the dough around the filling to enclose it completely, pinching the edges together to seal.
    • Flatten the stuffed dough ball slightly and roll it out into a larger circle, being careful not to let the filling spill out.
  3. Cook the Paratha:
    • Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat.
    • Place the rolled-out paratha on the hot griddle and cook for about 1-2 minutes, or until small bubbles start to form.
    • Flip the paratha and cook the other side for 1-2 minutes.
    • Brush a little oil or ghee on both sides of the paratha, and cook until golden brown and crispy, pressing gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking.
  4. Repeat:
    • Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  5. Serve:
    • erve the Aloo Parathas hot with yogurt, pickles, or a dollop of butter.
