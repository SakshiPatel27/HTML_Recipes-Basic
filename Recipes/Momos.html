MOMOS

Momos are a type of dumpling popular in South Asian cuisine, particularly in Nepal, Tibet, and parts of India.These bite-sized treats consist of a dough wrapper filled with a savory mixture, which can include vegetables, minced meat, or cheese. The dumplings are typically steamed, though they can also be fried or boiled.

Momos are often served with a spicy dipping sauce, such as a tangy tomato chutney or a spicy chili sauce. They are enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or part of a meal and are loved for their delicate, flavorful filling and soft, chewy exterior.

Ingredients

    For the Dough:

  • All-Purpose Flour: 2 cups
  • Water: Approximately 1 cup (adjust as needed)
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Oil: 1 tablespoon (optional, for softer dough)

    For the Filling:

  • Cabbage: 1 cup, finely chopped
  • Carrot: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Green Chili: 1, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • Soy Sauce: 1-2 tablespoons
  • Salt: To taste
  • Black Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Oil: 1 tablespoon (for cooking the vegetables)
  • Cilantro (Coriander Leaves): 2 tablespoons, finely chopped (optional, for added flavor)
  • Spring Onion: 2-3 tablespoons, finely chopped (optional, for added crunch)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    1. Mix Ingredients:
      • In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
      • Gradually add water (approximately 1 cup) and knead to form a smooth and elastic dough. Optionally, add 1 tablespoon of oil for a softer dough.
    2. Rest the Dough:
      • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    1. Chop Vegetables:
      • Finely chop 1 cup of cabbage, 1 medium carrot, and 1 small onion. Optionally, you can also use finely chopped green beans or bell peppers.
    2. Cook Vegetables:
      • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat.
      • Add 2-3 cloves of minced garlic, 1-inch piece of minced ginger, and 1-2 finely chopped green chilies (if using).
      • Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
      • Add the chopped vegetables and cook for about 5-7 minutes until they are tender but still crisp.
      • Season with 1-2 tablespoons of soy sauce, salt to taste, and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper.
      • Stir in 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro (optional).
      • Let the mixture cool.
  3. Shape the Momos:
    1. Divide the Dough:
      • Divide the rested dough into small balls, about the size of a walnut.
    2. Roll Out the Dough:
      • On a floured surface, roll each ball into a thin, round circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).
    3. Fill the Dough:
      • Place a small spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of each dough circle.
    4. Seal the Momos:
      • Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape or bring the edges together to create a pleated pouch.
      • Pinch the edges to seal the momo completely.
  4. Cook the Momos:
    1. Prepare the Steamer:
      • Heat water in a steamer or a large pot with a steaming rack.
    2. Arrange the Momos:
      • Place the momos on a parchment-lined steaming tray or rack, making sure they are not touching each other to prevent sticking.
    3. Steam the Momos:
      • Steam the momos over high heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is cooked and translucent.
  5. Serve:
    1. Serve Hot:
      • Remove the momos from the steamer and let them cool slightly.
    2. Accompaniments:
      • Serve the vegetable momos hot with dipping sauces such as spicy chili sauce, tomato chutney, or soy sauce.
