Manchurian

Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish known for its tangy and spicy flavors. It typically features deep-fried vegetable or meat balls, such as cauliflower (in the case of Gobi Manchurian) or chicken (in Chicken Manchurian), which are then coated in a savory, slightly sweet, and spicy sauce. The sauce is made with ingredients like soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, garlic, ginger, and sometimes a touch of sugar. Manchurian is often served as a starter or main course and is usually accompanied by fried rice or noodles. It’s a fusion dish that combines traditional Chinese ingredients with Indian cooking techniques and flavors.


Ingredients

    For the Manchurian Balls:

  • For Vegetable Manchurian: 1 cup finely chopped cauliflower (or a mix of finely chopped cabbage, carrots, and beans)
  • All-Purpose Flour: 2 tablespoons (for binding)
  • Cornstarch: 2 tablespoons
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Green Chili: 1-2, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • Salt: To taste
  • Black Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Oil: For deep frying

    For the Manchurian Sauce:

  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (for sautéing)
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, finely chopped
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, finely chopped
  • Green Chili: 1-2, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Bell Peppers: 1/2 cup, diced (optional)
  • Soy Sauce: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Vinegar: 1-2 tablespoons
  • Chili Sauce: 2 tablespoons (adjust to taste)
  • Tomato Ketchup: 1-2 tablespoons (for sweetness)
  • Cornstarch: 1 tablespoon (mixed with 2 tablespoons water to make a slurry)
  • Salt: To taste
  • Black Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Spring Onions: 2-3, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sesame Seeds: 1 tablespoon (optional, for garnish)

    For Garnishing (Optional):

  • Chopped Spring Onions: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Sesame Seeds: 1 tablespoon

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Manchurian Balls:
    1. Prepare the Vegetables:
      • Finely chop 1 cup of cauliflower (or a mix of finely chopped cabbage,carrots, and beans).
    2. Mix the Ingredients:
      • In a bowl, combine the chopped vegetables with 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of cornstarch, 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, and salt to taste.
      • Add finely chopped green chili (if using) for extra heat.
    3. Form the Balls:
      • Mix everything well. If the mixture is too dry, add a little water to bind it together. If too wet, add a bit more flour.
      • Shape the mixture into small, round balls.
  2. Prepare the Manchurian Sauce:
    1. Sauté Aromatics:
      • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan.
      • Add 4-5 finely chopped garlic cloves, 1-inch piece of finely chopped ginger, and 1-2 finely chopped green chilies (if using).
      • Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    2. Cook Vegetables:
      • Add 1 medium finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
      • If using, add 1/2 cup diced bell peppers and cook for another 2 minutes.
    3. Add Sauces:
      • Add 2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1-2 tablespoons of vinegar, 2 tablespoons of chili sauce, and 1-2 tablespoons of tomato ketchup.
      • Stir to combine and cook for 1-2 minutes.
    4. Thicken the Sauce:
      • Add the cornstarch slurry (1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water) to the pan.
      • Stir well and cook until the sauce thickens. Adjust seasoning with salt and black pepper.
  3. Combine and Serve:
    1. Add Manchurian Balls:
      • Add the fried Manchurian balls (vegetable or chicken) to the sauce.
      • Gently toss to coat the balls evenly with the sauce.
    2. Garnish:
      • Garnish with chopped spring onions and sesame seeds if desired.
    3. Serve:
      • Serve hot with fried rice or noodles.
